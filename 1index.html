Fact 1: Blood Volume

An average adult has about 4–5 liters of blood.

This makes up about 7–8% of body weight, equivalent to ~13 soda cans.[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://www.factretriever.com/blood-facts?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=1 "FactRetriever")

Fact 2: Blood Components

Blood is made up of plasma, red cells, white cells, and platelets.

Plasma is ~90% water and carries nutrients; red cells transport oxygen; white cells fight infection; platelets help clotting.[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://facts.net/earth-and-life-science/biology-earth-and-life-science/36-facts-about-blood/?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=2 "Facts.net")

Fact 3: Blood Cell Production

Bone marrow produces millions of blood cells every second.

About 2 million red blood cells are created each second to replace worn-out cells.[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://www.factretriever.com/blood-facts?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=1 "FactRetriever")

Fact 4: Blood Functions

Blood delivers oxygen and nutrients, removes waste, and regulates temperature.

It also helps fight infections and maintains stable internal conditions.[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://facts.net/earth-and-life-science/biology-earth-and-life-science/36-facts-about-blood/?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=2 "Facts.net")

Fact 5: Blood is a Tissue

Blood is classified as connective tissue, not just a fluid.

Because it contains living cells, blood is considered a tissue with structural and functional roles.[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://www.factretriever.com/blood-facts?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=1 "FactRetriever")
